Excellence In and Out of the Classroom

ABAC Academics

Education is at its best when classroom study meets real-world experience. At ABAC, we pride ourselves on offering an experiential, personalized approach to learning that gives graduates both the theoretical framework and the hands-on practice they need to thrive in their endeavors. Our focus on integrity both in and out of the classroom means that Stallions leave ABAC with a goal of making the world a better place.

With small class sizes, an 18:1 student-to-faculty ratio, and degree programs focused on providing an interactive learning environment building towards a workforce-driven credential, ABAC’s four schools prepare students for a successful career after graduation.

Bainbridge Site

While ABAC’s home remains in the thriving rural community of Tifton, our additional instruction site for Southwest Georgia in Bainbridge offers students another option for starting their journey in higher education.

The Key to Our Academic Excellence: ABAC Faculty

Our nationally-recognized faculty are the core of our academic offerings. These dedicated members of the ABAC family bring to the table not only their unmatched experience and knowledge, but also a passion for teaching and helping students explore their interests and find the way towards a rewarding and fulfilling future.

Explore Our Schools

Each of ABAC’s academic programs is housed in one of our four schools. Once you’ve chosen your degree, certificate, or minor, your school will become your academic “home base,” where you’ll find your advisor, most of your faculty members, and an incredible network of support in the fellow students who share your passion for your field of study.

Office of the Provost

The Provost and Vice President for Academic Affairs (VPAA) is the College’s Chief Academic Officer, and is ultimately responsible for all academic and student affairs matters. The Provost is responsible for:

  • Leading ABAC’s faculty
  • Planning and implementing academic and student policy
  • Providing guidance for the curriculum development
  • Promoting core values and academic integrity
  • Fostering academic excellence
